The Sun is composed primarily of Hydrogen (74.9%) and Helium (23.8%) and a few other heavier elements (metals) like Oxygen, Carbon, Iron etc. This proportion changes over time as the Sun converts Hydrogen to Helium in its core through Nuclear Fusion.

Like Earth, Sun also rotates about its axis. Its sidereal rotation takes 25.05 days to complete at the equator and about 35 days at the poles. Why such a differential rotation? Because Sun is a molten ball of fire. The fluids near the poles move progressively slower than the fluids at the equator.

Apart from sidereal rotation, the Sun along with the Solar System orbits around the Galactic Center (GC) of the Milky Way. At a staggering velocity of 514,000 mph! It is estimated that a full revolution takes about 240 million terrestrial (Earth) years. Given that Sun is only ~4.57 billion years old it has been around the GC only 20 times so far.
