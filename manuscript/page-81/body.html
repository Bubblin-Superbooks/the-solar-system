Saturn is the sixth planet of our Solar System. It is also the second largest planet, a gas-giant that is very similar to Jupiter. Saturn is 890 million miles from the Sun. It has an orbital period of 29.5 years and a rotational period of just under 10 hours 34 minutes. Given the high speed of its rotation and gaseous-fluid make up Saturn is oblate-spheroid in shape. It is in fact the flattest of all the planets in the Solar System.

What's remarkable about Saturn are its large, concentric and colorful rings. Other gas and ice giants also possess planetary rings, but none can match the size or the beauty of the ones on Saturn. Exotic rings are also the reason why Saturn is called the Jewel of The Heavens. Saturn has a total of 62 moons of which Titan and Rhea are the most notable. Titan, like the Ganymede of Jupiter, is larger than the planet Mercury.
