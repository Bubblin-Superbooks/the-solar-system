Moon takes 27.32 days to go one full round around the Earth. It also rotates about its own axis in roughly the same time viz. 27.32 days. This is called synchronous rotation, a phenomena that explains why we see only one side — the near side — of the Moon (See a visual explanation overleaf).

Synchronous rotation is caused by tidal locking of Moon to Earth. This locking has a great influence on the life on Earth. For example, it causes oceanic buldging, causes low and high tides, stabilizes Earth's rotational axis and even decelerates Earth's rotation due to tidal braking.

There's a school of thought that believes that complex life on Earth wouldn't have been possible without the Moon. Note both Moon and Sun are visible to our naked eye and both do not look like starry dots in the sky.
