Earth has strong magnetic and gravitational fields. It has a natural satellite, Moon, about a quarter in diameter and roughly 239,000 miles away. Moon has a great influence on Earth. It causes the tides, oceanic bulging and tidal braking — a phenomenon that slows Earth's rotational velocity and stabilizes it, and is considered critical for life.

Rotation

Earth rotates about its axis every 24 hours. Rotational velocity at its equator is about 1000 mph; that's quite fast, almost matching the top speed of the latest fighterjet F-22 Raptor! Earth spins from the West to East so in relative terms it appears to us that the Sun rises in the East and sets in the West.

The internal axis of Earth's rotation is tilted at 23.5 degrees from perpendicular to the plane of our orbit — a tilt that causes our seasons.
