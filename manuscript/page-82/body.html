Saturn is similar to Jupiter in composition. Its atmosphere is 96% Hydrogen and 3.25% Helium followed by traces of Ammonia, Phosphene and a few other lower hydrocarbons. The interior of Saturn, unlike that of Jupiter, is known to have a solid core at its center. A rocky core enveloped in a layer of metallic hydrogen followed by molecular hydrogen transitioning gradually from liquid to gaseous state of atmosphere. NASA's space probe Cassini is currently in orbit around Saturn for further research on the planet.
