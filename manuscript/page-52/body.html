Venus has a heavy and opaque atmosphere, laden with 92-95% CO2 and a thick layer of clouds of sulfuric acid that absorb most of the radiant heat from Sun. For the greenhouse effect of its atmosphere, Venus is the hottest planet in the Solar System.

Like Earth, Venus too has an inner core, a solid mantle and a crust followed by an atmosphere. But it is a lifeless planet and is concluded to be geologically too harsh to ever sustain Earth-like life; despite the idea of terraforming.
