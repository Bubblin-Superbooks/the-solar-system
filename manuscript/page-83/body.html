Observations have also detected discharge of seasalts and clay-like silicious material on its surface. These are signs often associated with organic microbial activity. Measurements suggest that a portion of atmospheric Oxygen makes its way into the ocean of Europa, making conditions there comparable to the deep seas on Earth. Given Europa's potential for life, two major missions have been planned to visit the moon in the coming years: The NASA's Europa Clipper and ESA's JUICE.
