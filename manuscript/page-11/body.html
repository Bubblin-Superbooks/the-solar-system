Introduction

It has taken more than a thousand years and courage to figure out what our Solar System looks like. And how it works. Up until the sixteenth century we thought there were only five planets out there and that Earth was at the center of the Universe (see a dynamic visualization overleaf). That Moon, Mercury, Venus, Sun, Mars, Jupiter and Saturn revolved around Earth in a series of succeeding celestial spheres, and strictly in that order.
