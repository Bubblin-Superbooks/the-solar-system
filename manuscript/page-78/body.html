Jupiter has a very faint ring system. This ring is not visible to an unaided eye from Earth. The Jovian rings were discovered by Voyager I only in 1979 and are mainly made of dust particles, presumably collected from impacts, or rather rub-off of incoming comets and asteroids. The exact mechanism of how rings are formed is not understood yet.
