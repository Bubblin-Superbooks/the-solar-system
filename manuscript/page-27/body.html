Now that we have a little primer on Gravitational Physics let's take a close look at the Sun. Sun is a star at the center of our Solar System. It doesn't look quite like other stars at night but it is one of them. Sun outshines all the other stars, rather turns our night sky into a bright sunny day!, only because it is very close to us. From an astronomical standpoint however, it is only an unremarkable young medium-sized star informally classified as a Yellow Dwarf.

There are other stars that are larger, older and brighter than the Sun. The Sun accounts for about 99.86% of the total mass of the Solar System. Kind of explains why every planet goes around it with Sun remaining nearly fixed at the C.O.M of the system — we'd seen this earlier in our Earth-Sun gravitation model too! Sun is about as old as the Solar System (≈ 4.57 billion years) and is roughly halfway through its estimated nucleocosmochronological lifespan.
