Broadly speaking a lot depends on our definition and scope of terraforming and what we want to achieve from these off-earth settlements. Today when terraforming is still in theory and we barely have the means to manage our own environmental changes here on Earth, it'd be safe to assume that colonization is still far away. At least few decades before we can realize what can be achieved outside the Earth.

Secondly, there is always a chance to find an extra-solar equal of Earth. If that happens, focus will shift from transforming dead hosts to better and faster travel. Or to find alien life at the new host. It is very likely that such a celestial body is already a host to diverse organic life.
