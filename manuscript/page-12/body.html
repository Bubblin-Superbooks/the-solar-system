He argued not only that the Earth moved, but also that it moved in two different ways — diurnal motion on its axis, and annual motion around the Sun. The book soon turned into a revolution that is now considered a major event in the history of Science and Technology.

The heliocentric model triggered a revolution, the Copernican Revolution, that led to more work and many more discoveries in the field of astronomy over the next one century. Johannes Kepler (1571 - 1630), a German scientist, concluded that planets moved in elliptical orbits and not in circular or epicyclic ones.
