De Revolutionibus Orbium Coelestium in 1543. Copernicus suggested that Sun was stationary at the center of everything else instead of Earth—ala, the Heliocentric model. He also put the planets in their correct order i.e. Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter and then Saturn. He argued that not only the Earth moved, but it also did so in two different ways—in a diurnal motion, about its axis, and in annual motion around the Sun. His book soon turned into a revolution that is now considered a major event in the history of Science and Technology as we know it.

The heliocentric model triggered a revolution, the Copernican Revolution, that led to more work and many more discoveries in the field of astronomy over the next one century.
