Galileo Galilei (1564 - 1642), the father of modern observational astronomy and science, discovered the Phases of Venus through his telescope and documented Jupiter's Galilean Moons for the very first time. These observations supported the Copernican cosmology over its predecessors and led to Galileo's house arrest for life.

The Copernican Revolution came to a close only after the formulation of the Three Laws of Motion and the Universal Law of Gravitation by Sir Isaac Newton in the seventeenth century.
